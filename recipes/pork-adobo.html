Pork Sinigang

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish. In this recipe, we'll be using pork. Pork slices are cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and onions.

There are different renditions of adobo. Chicken adobo is the more popular version of this dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or liempo (whichever you prefer) - chopped into smaller pieces
  • 1 bulb onion - chopped
  • 5-6 cloves of garlic - minced
  • 4-5 pieces bay leaves or laurel
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 4 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon peppercorn
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Marinade the pork belly (or pork liempo) in the mixture of soy sauce and minced garlic for at least an hour.

    1 kg pork belly or pork liempo, 1/2 cup soy sauce, minced garlic

  2. Saute the onion and minced garlic. Once golden, add the marinated pork. Saute for a few minutes until golden brown.

    3. Marinated pork, 1 piece onion, 5-6 cloves of garlic

  3. Once the meat is golden brown, pour the marinade.
  4. Add water, whole peppercorns, and dried bay leaves. Bring to a boil. Simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour.

    5. 2 cups water, 1 tablespoon peppercorn, 5 dried bay leaves

  5. Add vinegar and simmer for another 12 to 15 minutes.

    6. 4 tablespoons vinegar

  6. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot. Enjoy!
